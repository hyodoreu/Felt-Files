Hyodoreu’s Woolly Journey

It all started in 2021 — I took a break from uni and got into felting for about a week. Made a few mushrooms just for fun.

Fast forward to 2024, I used wool felt in my grad exhibition as part of the illustration track. That’s when I got really into turning my own sketches into soft 3D forms by hand.

Since then, I’ve been slowly making weird and cute things, one at a time.

Made 24 wool felt characters for my grad exhibition! Some were almost my height, others could fit in the palm of my hand. Took a little over a month to finish everything. Was juggling grad show committee duties and two exhibition tracks at the same time… Almost didn’t make it. Literally finished felting the night before the install!

This one’s a Japanese bonsai.

Made it super last minute — sketched it real quick and pulled an all-nighter to finish.It was for a grad committee MT, meant to be gifted randomly to someone. Turned out way better than expected, honestly. There’s just something about the shape of bonsai that I find really cool, so I still make them every now and then.

Sketched some round little characters and thought, "wait… these would be cute in wool. "So I made them. Their chunky bellies and bouncy butts? Definitely part of the charm. I also played around with different hats — had way too much fun with that. One felt too lonely, so of course, I made a bunch.

Made this card wallet after watching a wet felting tutorial on YouTube. Tried buttonhole stitching for the first time — thick thread + wool felt? Kinda loved it.

*real talk: wet felt is trickier than it looks and eats up way more wool than expected.

Found a snail on the rooftop.

Instantly thought—this would be so cute in needle felt! The shell color? Insane.

It’s got that aqua vibe, and I used Japanese Hamanaka acrylic wool for that part. The rest is all natural wool Making those tiny little feelers was oddly satisfying too~

Got some wool balls lying around at home, and when you bunch them up—boom! Adorable, super-textured font vibes. Ended up making like 40 wool balls and turned them into a font. My fave letters? a, g, and t!
